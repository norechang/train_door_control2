# Change Control Board (CCB) Charter

**Project:** train_door_control2  
**SIL Level:** 3  
**Date:** 2026-02-18  

---

## 1. Purpose

The Change Control Board (CCB) is established to manage all changes to the Train Door Control System software in accordance with EN 50128:2011 Section 6.6 (Modification and Change Control) and Section 5.3.2.13 (Phase Modification Authority).

The CCB ensures that all changes are:
- Properly evaluated for impact
- Approved by authorized personnel
- Traced through the lifecycle
- Compliant with safety requirements

---

## 2. Authority and Responsibilities

### 2.1 Authority
The CCB has authority to:
- Approve or reject change requests
- Authorize phase modifications (with COD approval)
- Establish change implementation priorities
- Require additional analysis or testing for changes

### 2.2 Responsibilities
The CCB shall:
- Review all change requests within 5 business days
- Evaluate change impact on safety, schedule, and cost
- Ensure traceability of changes to requirements
- Verify that changes comply with EN 50128 techniques
- Coordinate with Validator for safety-critical changes
- Maintain change control records
- Report change status to Project Manager

## 4. Meeting Schedule

- **Regular Meetings**: Bi-weekly (every 2 weeks)
- **Emergency Meetings**: Within 24 hours for critical safety issues
- **Ad-hoc Meetings**: As needed for urgent changes

---

## 5. Change Request Process

### 5.1 Change Request Submission
- Submit change requests via Configuration Manager
- Use standard CR form (CR-001)
- Include impact assessment
- Classify by priority (Low/Medium/High/Critical)

### 5.2 Review Process
1. **Initial Review** (Secretary): Validate completeness within 1 business day
2. **Impact Assessment** (Technical Leads): Evaluate technical/safety/quality impact
3. **CCB Review**: Formal review and decision
4. **Implementation**: Approved changes only

### 5.3 Approval Criteria
Changes are approved if:
- No negative safety impact (SIL 3 requirement)
- Within project scope and budget
- Adequate testing planned
- Traceability maintained
- Validator concurrence for safety changes

### 5.4 Escalation
- Rejected changes may be escalated to Safety Authority
- Critical safety issues escalated immediately

---

## 6. Decision Making

### 6.1 Voting
- **Consensus preferred** for all decisions
- **Majority vote** if consensus not reached
- **Chair has tie-breaking vote**
- **Safety concerns override other considerations**

### 6.2 Decision Types
- **Approve**: Change implemented as proposed
- **Approve with Conditions**: Change approved with modifications
- **Reject**: Change not approved
- **Defer**: Additional information required

---

## 7. Documentation and Reporting

### 7.1 Records
- All change requests and decisions recorded
- Meeting minutes distributed within 1 business day
- Change history maintained in version control

### 7.2 Reporting
- Monthly change summary to Project Manager
- Quarterly reports to Safety Authority
- Immediate notification of critical changes

---

## 8. Compliance Requirements

### 8.1 EN 50128 Compliance
- Section 6.6: Modification and change control (MANDATORY)
- Section 5.3.2.13: Phase modification authority
- Section 6.5: Quality assurance integration

### 8.2 SIL 3 Requirements
- All changes evaluated for safety impact
- Independent Validator involvement for safety changes
- Traceability maintained end-to-end
- Formal change control process
